'Cooking Oil' is plant, animal, or synthetic fat used in frying, baking, and other types of cooking. It is also used in food preparation and flavoring not involving head such as salad dressings and bread dips, and in this sense might be more accurately termed edible oil.
Cooking oil is typically a liquid at room temperature, although some oils contain saturated fat such as coconut oil. Oil can be flavoured with aromatic foodstuffs such as herbs, chillies or garlic. All oils degrade in response to heat, ight and oxygen. In a cool dry place, oils have greater stability but may thicken, although they will soon return to liquid if they are left at room temperature.

AVOCADO OIL

Avocado oil is an edible oil pressed from the avocado fruit. As a food oil, it is used as an ingredient in other dishes, and as a cooking oil. It is also used for lubrication and in cosmetics, where it is valued for its supposed regenerative and moisturizing properties. It is pressedfrom the fleshy pulp surrounding the avocado pit and not the pit itself.
Avocado oil is one of the few edible oils not derived from ots seeds; it is pressed from the fleshy pulp surrounding the avocado pit.

COCONUT OIL

Coconut oil is an edible oil extracted from the hernel or meat of mature coconuuts harvested from the coconut palm.
Coconut oil is 99% fat, composed mainly of saturated fats. In a 100g reference amount, coconut oil supplies 890 calories.
Coconut oil has been used for hair grooming, and has been found to reduce protein loss in hair. It has also been used as an important base ingredient in the manufacture of soap.

PEANUT OIL

Peanut oil, also known as groundnut oil is a mild-tasting vegetable oil derived from peanuts. The oil has a strong peanut flavor and aroma.
Its major component fatty acids are oleic acid, linoleic acid and palmitic acid. The oil also contains some stearic acid, behenic acid and other fatty acids.
A 100g of peanut oil contains 17.7g of saturated fat, 48.3g of monounsaturated fat and 33.4g of polyunsaturated fat.
