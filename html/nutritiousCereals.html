'Cereals' are grasses cultivated for the edibile components of thier grain. Cereals are a rich source of vitamins, minerals, carbonhydrates, fats, oils and protein. The warm season cereals are grown in tropical lowlands year-round and in temperate climates during the frost free season. Cool season cereals are more well adapted to temperate climates. Winter varieties do not flower until springtime because they require vernalization: exposure to low temperatures for a genetically determined length of time. Once the cereal plants have completed thier life cycle. The plants die, become brown and dry.

MAIZE

Maize also known as corn is a cereal grain first domesticated by the indigenous people in southern mexico about 10,000 years ago.
Maize has become a staple food in many parts of the world, with the total production of maize surpassing that of wheat or rice.
Maize is widely cultivated throughout the world, and a greater weight of maize is produced each year than any other grain.

POACEAE / GRAMINAE

Poaceae or Graminae is a large and nearly ubiquitous family of monocotyledonous flowering plants known as grasses, commonly reffered to as grass. Pocae includes the cereal classes bamboos and the grasses of natural grassland and are cultivated by lawns and pasture.
Pocacae are the most economically important plant family, providing staple food for domesticated cereal crops such as maize, wheat, rice, barley etc.
