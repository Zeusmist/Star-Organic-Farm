The juice vesicles aka pulp of a citrus fruit are the membranous content of the fruit's endocarp. All fruits from the citrus family have juice vessicles.
The vesicles contain the juice of the fruit and appear shiny and baglike. Juice vesicles of the endocarp contain the eomponents thet provide the aroma typically with the citrus fruit.
They are also very large and protect the seeds of the fruit. The color of the pulp varies depending on the species and the ripening stage. Usually it has the color of the outer peel.

ORANGE

Orange is the fruit of the citrus species, native to China. The orange is a hybrid between pomelo and mandarin. The chloroplast genome, and material line, is that of pomelo. The sweet orange has its full genome sequenced.
As a citrus fruit, the orange is acidic, with pH levels ranging from 2.9 to 4.0.
As with other citrus fruits, orange pulp is an excellent source of vitamin C, providing 64% of the daily value in a 100g serving.

PINEAPPLE

Pineapple is a tropical plant with an edible fruit also called a pineapple, and the most economically significant part in the family Bromeliaceae.
The pineapple is a herbaceous perennial which grows up to 1.0 to 1.5m tall.
The flesh and juice of the pineapple are used in cusines around the world. In many tropical countries, pineapples are prepared nd sold on roadsides as snacks.

WATERMELON

Watermelon is a scrambling and trailing vine in the flowering plant Cucurbitacae. The watermelon is an annual that has a prostrate or climbing habit. Stems are up to 3m long and new growth has yellow or brown hairs.
Thier seeds have a nutty flavor and can be dried and roasted, or ground into flour.
Watermelon fruit is 91% water, contains 6% sugars and is low in fat. In a 100g serving, watermelon supplies 30 calories.
