'A Spice' is a seed, fruit,root, bark or other plant substance primarily using for flavoring, coloring or preserving food. Many spices have antimicrobial properties i.e (they inhibit the growth of harmful bacterial).
Spices are sometimes used in medicine, religious rituals, cosmetics or perfume production. A spice may be available in several forms: fresh, whole dried, or pre-ground dried.
Generally, spices are dried. Spices may be ground into a powder for convenience.

GINGER

Ginger is a flowering plant whose rhizome is widely used as a spice and a folk medicine.
Ginger produces a hot, fragrant kitchen spice. Young ginger rhizomes are juicy and fleshy with mild taste. They are often packed in vinegar or sherry as a snack or cooked as an ingredient in many dishes.
Raw ginger is composed of 79% water, 18% carbonhydrates, 2% protein and 1% fat.

CINNAMON

Cinnamon is a spice obtained from the inner bark of several tree species from the genus Cinnamomum. It is mostly used as an aromatic condiment and flavouring additive in a wide variety of cusines, sweet and savoury dishes, breakfast cereals, snackfoods, tea and traditional foods. The tern "cinnamon" is also used to describe its mid-brown color.
In the U.S cinnamon is often used to flavour cereals and bread based dishes such as toast.

BLACK PEPPER

Black pepper is a flowering vine in the family Piperaceae, cultivated for its fruit, known as a peppercorn, which is usually dried and used as a spice and seasoning.
One tablespoon of ground black pepper contains moderate amounts of vitamin k, iron and maganese, with trace amounts of other essential nutrients, protein and dietary fiber.
